Process (CPU)

What does it do?

The CPU (Central Processing Unit) is the brain of your computer.
Every action you take opening apps, typing, gaming gets processed by the CPU.

What to look out for?

Core Count

Think of cores as workers. More workers = more tasks handled at once.

Clock Speed (GHz)

How fast each worker works. Higher GHz = faster performance.

Spec Range by Use Case

Office Use (Excel, Word)
  • 2–4 cores, around 2.5–3.5 GHz
  • Example: Intel Core i3, AMD Ryzen 3
Gaming
  • 6–8 cores, around 3.6–5.0 GHz
  • Example: Intel Core i5/i7, AMD Ryzen 5/7
Video Editing / Content Creation
  • 10–16 cores, around 3.5–4.5 GHz
  • Example: Intel Core i9, AMD Ryzen 9
